Who am I?

I'm a tech and science enthusiast since very young age.
Later I focused my passion mainly on physics and informatics.
I started my freelance career before completing college studies in physics then I come back and took my Master Degree with honours.
But academic research was too slow for my needs so informatics became my career path.
I started as Linux system administrator and network specialist and later on I moved to software development.
In late 2000s I was searching for bigger ventures but remote jobs wasn't much of a thing and relocating was out of my possibilities.
With a good idea, after more than a year of struggle with bureaucracy, in 2011 a friend and I founded a start-up.
After a good start things started to go south and in 2015 we shut it down for good.
In 2016 I entered in Food s.r.l., a media company that wanted greatly accelerate its digital transition. I was assigned with tech lead and tasked to build a team and shape the technological backbone of the company.
4 years later company's digital offer, internal automation and data integration expanded many fold continuing to spin up new projects and functionalities.

How do I approach software development?

I unearth needs and opportunities under requests and problems.
I summarize them clearing ambiguities and vagueness as much as possible.
I elaborate possibly radically different solutions and for each one I present tradeoffs together with my advices.
I develop the chosen solution by myself or coordinating other developers and suppliers.
In latest years I focused on architectural choices, backend development and management but when needed I develop frontend too.
I like to dirt my hands at solving problems.
